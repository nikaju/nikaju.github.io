Astronomy Projects

Nuclear regions as seen with LOFAR international baselines. A high-resolution study of the recurrent activity

Who? Lead researcher: Nika Jurlin, collaboration with LOFAR team

When? 2023–2024

Why? To study radio emission from restarted galaxies using multi-frequency data

Take-away message: Evidence for multiple cycles of AGN activity

Why important? Helps understand galaxy evolution and the AGN feedback loop

The Photometric and Spectroscopic Properties of Remnant and Restarted Radio Galaxies in the Lockman Hole Field

Who? PI: Nika Jurlin, co-authors from Netherlands and Italy

When? 2022

Why? Cross-analyze VLA and Hubble data for the CLASSY galaxy sample

Take-away message: CLASSY galaxies show unusually strong radio emission

Why important? Suggests link between starburst phases and radio activity
