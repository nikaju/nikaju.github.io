Astronomy Projects

Nuclear regions as seen with LOFAR international baselines. A high-resolution study of the recurrent activity

Who? PI: Nika Jurlin; collaborators from Italy, the Netherlands, and UK

When? Published in 2024

Why? To investigate nuclear radio structures in candidate restarted AGN using LOFAR's highest resolution images

Take-away message: Compact nuclear emission reveals possible restarted jets and sub-parsec scale structures

Why important? Provides insight into the duty cycle of AGN and mechanisms of radio re-triggering

The Photometric and Spectroscopic Properties of Remnant and Restarted Radio Galaxies in the Lockman Hole Field

Who? PI: Nika Jurlin; collaborators from The Netherlands and Italy

When? Published in 2021

Why? To understand whether there are differences in host galaxies of AGN in different stages of their lives

Take-away message: No significant differences found between host galaxies of radio AGN in different phases

Why important? Confirms these are different stages of galaxies hosting a black hole

Multi-frequency characterisation of remnant radio galaxies in the Lockman Hole field

Who? PI: Nika Jurlin; collaborators from Germany, India, Italy, and The Netherlands

When? Published in 2021

Why? To distinguish remnant AGN using radio continuum properties across multiple frequencies

Take-away message: Many remnants are compact and only detectable at low frequencies

Why important? Refines understanding of remnant AGN visibility and spectral evolution

The life cycle of radio galaxies in the LOFAR Lockman Hole field

Who? PI: Nika Jurlin; team includes experts from Australia, France, Germany, Italy, The Netherlands, South Africa, and the UK

When? Published in 2020

Why? To analyze morphological and spectral properties of AGN at various evolutionary stages

Take-away message: Introduces the first statistical sample of radio galaxies in different stages of their lives

Why important? Lays groundwork for statistical studies of AGN life cycles in deep radio fields
